Learn About Augmented Reality

Understand the fundamentals of AR technology and its impact.

What is Augmented Reality (AR)?

Augmented Reality is a technology that overlays digital content — such as images, models, or information — onto the real world through a device like a smartphone or AR headset. Unlike Virtual Reality, AR does not replace the real world; instead, it enhances it.

Types of AR

  • Marker-based AR: Uses image recognition (e.g., QR codes, printed images) to trigger content.
  • Markerless AR: Uses GPS, accelerometer, or SLAM (simultaneous localization and mapping) to detect environments.
  • Face-based AR: Used in filters (e.g., Instagram/Snapchat) for face tracking and enhancement.

Applications of AR

AR is used in a wide variety of fields: education, gaming, retail, healthcare, architecture, and more. It helps users interact with virtual elements in the context of the physical world.
